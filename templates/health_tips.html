Diet & Nutrition

Focus on heart-healthy foods: Incorporate more fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats into your diet.

Reduce saturated and trans fats: Limit intake of red meat, full-fat dairy, and fried foods to help manage cholesterol levels.

Lower sodium consumption: Aim for less than 2,300mg of sodium per day to help manage blood pressure.

Increase fiber intake: Consume 25-30g of fiber daily through foods like oats, beans, and fruits to help lower cholesterol.

Choose healthy fats: Opt for olive oil, avocados, nuts, and fatty fish rich in omega-3 fatty acids.

Physical Activity

Regular aerobic exercise: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity activity per week, such as brisk walking, swimming, or cycling.

Strength training: Include muscle-strengthening activities at least twice a week to improve overall cardiovascular health.

Daily movement: Even small amounts of physical activity throughout the day can benefit your heart - take the stairs, walk during breaks.

Consistency over intensity: Regular, moderate exercise is often more beneficial than occasional intense workouts.

Find activities you enjoy: You're more likely to stick with exercise routines that you find pleasurable.

Lifestyle Choices

Quit smoking: Smoking damages blood vessels and significantly increases heart disease risk - quitting can rapidly improve heart health.

Limit alcohol: If you drink, do so in moderation (no more than 1 drink daily for women, 2 for men).

Maintain healthy weight: Achieving a healthy BMI can reduce strain on your heart and lower disease risk.

Manage stress: Chronic stress contributes to heart disease - practice relaxation techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, or yoga.

Prioritize sleep: Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night, as poor sleep is linked to increased heart disease risk.

Medical Management

Regular check-ups: Schedule routine health screenings to monitor blood pressure, cholesterol, and blood sugar levels.

Know your numbers: Understand your key health metrics and work with healthcare providers to keep them in healthy ranges.

Medication adherence: If prescribed medications for heart health, take them as directed by your healthcare provider.

Manage underlying conditions: Effectively treat conditions like diabetes, high blood pressure, and high cholesterol that contribute to heart disease.

Family history awareness: If heart disease runs in your family, be proactive about prevention and early intervention.

Key Risk Factors for Heart Disease

High Blood Pressure

Aim for levels below 120/80 mmHg

High Cholesterol

Target total cholesterol under 200 mg/dL

Diabetes

Maintain HbA1c below 7% if diabetic

Smoking

Quitting reduces risk by up to 50% in one year

Obesity

Aim for a BMI between 18.5-24.9

Physical Inactivity

Even 30 minutes daily makes a difference
